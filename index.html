STEPS Decision Aid Tool

STEPS helps policy makers in India plan and scale Field Epidemiology Training Programs by forecasting programme uptake, evaluating economic tradeoffs, and comparing key programme features.

Field Epidemiology Training Programs (FETP) are essential for strengthening public health responses. How to Use STEPS

  1. Review Attributes: The Attributes tab explains each key feature using visually appealing info icons with detailed descriptions.
  2. Configure Inputs: In the Inputs tab, select options from each compact block. Reference values (in parentheses) guide your selections.
  3. Program Adoption Likelihood: View the donut chart which forecasts the probability that policy makers endorse your configuration.
  4. Costs & Benefits: The analysis displays the Total Training Cost, Monetised Benefits, Net Benefit, and shows both full capacity and the Potential Effective Enrollment (capacity adjusted by Predicted Uptake). For example, if capacity is 500 and uptake is 70%, effective enrollment is 350.
  5. Saved Scenarios: Save and export scenario details individually or as an overall PDF for side-by-side comparisons.

Assumptions & Explanations

Total Cost Calculation: Total Cost = Fixed Costs + (Fee per Training Completion √ó Annual Capacity). Fixed Costs begin at $35,500 with a $10 adjustment per trainee difference from 500, plus additional costs based on delivery method and training level.

Benefits Calculation: Monetised Benefits = (QALY Gain per Participant √ó Enrollment √ó $50,000). For example, if QALY Gain is 0.05 and Annual Capacity is 500, then without uptake adjustment benefits equal 0.05 √ó 500 √ó $50,000 = $1,250,000. With a Predicted Uptake of 70%, Effective Enrollment becomes 350, yielding benefits of 0.05 √ó 350 √ó $50,000 = $875,000.
