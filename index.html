STEPS Decision Aid Tool

STEPS helps policy makers in India plan and scale Field Epidemiology Training Programs by forecasting programme uptake, evaluating economic tradeoffs, and comparing key programme features.

Field Epidemiology Training Programs (FETP) are key to strengthening public health responses. STEPS uses discrete choice experiment estimates to forecast programme uptake – meaning the predicted likelihood that decision makers will endorse a given training configuration – and to assess cost–benefit tradeoffs.

  • Instructions – A visually engaging guide to using the tool.
  • Attributes – A snapshot of the main programme features.
  • Inputs – Configure your programme options with clear, interactive controls.
  • Adoption Likelihood – View a forecast of decision maker support.
  • Costs & Benefits – Explore a detailed, dynamic economic summary.
  • Visualizations – Interact with maps and dashboards showing regional capacities and cost distributions.
  • Saved Scenarios – Save, compare, and export your configurations.

How to Use STEPS

  1. Review Attributes: View a quick overview of the key programme features.
  2. Configure Inputs: In the Inputs tab, select options such as Level of Training, Training Model, Stipend, Capacity, Delivery Method, Number of Training Sites, and Fee. Reference values (in parentheses) guide your selection.
  3. Adoption Likelihood: Check the Adoption Likelihood tab to see the forecasted support from decision makers.
  4. Costs & Benefits: Examine the economic summary. Toggle the Cost Breakdown and Benefits Explanation sections for detailed insights.
  5. Visualizations: Explore interactive maps and a dashboard that illustrate regional capacities and cost distributions.
  6. Saved Scenarios: Save your configurations and export comparisons as a PDF.

Costs & Benefits Analysis

This section estimates the total programme cost and monetised benefits.
Benefits = (QALY Gain per Participant × Number of Trainees × $50,000). This approximates the economic value of improved health outcomes.

Assumptions

Cost estimates combine variable costs (participant fee) and fixed costs (venue, materials, trainer fees, etc.). A base fixed cost of $35,500 is adjusted by $10 for each trainee above or below 500.

Direct Costs

  • Training & Development:
    • Venue rental for workshops and meetings
    • Acquisition of training materials
    • Compensation for trainers and speakers
    • Content development and licensing fees
  • Personnel Costs:
    • Salaries for full-time staff
    • Consultant fees
    • Employment benefits and taxes
  • Technology Costs:
    • Hardware procurement
    • Software purchases
    • Maintenance and support
  • Logistical Costs:
    • Travel and accommodation expenses
    • Catering services
  • Miscellaneous Costs:
    • Printing, stationery, office supplies
    • Communication expenses
    • Insurance coverage

Indirect Costs

  • Opportunity Costs:
    • Lost productivity due to training participation
    • Temporary replacement or overtime expenses
  • Infrastructure Costs:
    • Upgrades to facilities
    • Depreciation of equipment
  • Administrative Costs:
    • Utilities and office space
    • Management time

Recurring Costs

  • Maintenance and Upkeep:
    • Regular updates to training materials
    • Continuous trainer development
    • Renewal fees for software/licenses

QALY Benefits

Benefits = (QALY Gain per Participant × Number of Trainees × $50,000). This reflects the economic value of improved health outcomes.
